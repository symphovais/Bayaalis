### **Step 1: Marinate the Beef**  
1. In a large bowl, combine:  
   - 1.5 kg Beef (with bones)  
   - 340 gm Curd (plain yogurt)  
   - 150 gm Fried onions (crushed)  
   - 115 gm Ginger garlic paste  
   - 4 tbsp Fried onion oil  
   - 4 Green chilies (mild, slit)  
   - 1-2 Green chilies (spicy, slit)  
   - 23 gm Degi red chili powder  
   - 23 gm Coriander powder  
   - 5 gm Caraway seeds (Shahi jeera)  
   - 15 gm Fresh mint leaves (roughly chopped)  
   - 2 tbsp Tender coriander stems (chopped)  
   - 35 gm Salt  

2. Mix well to coat the beef evenly. Cover and marinate for **at least 2 hours** (overnight for best results).  

---

### **Step 2: Prepare the Biryani Masala**  
Dry roast and grind together:  
   - 15 gm Coriander seeds  
   - 8 gm Cumin seeds  
   - 23 gm Black peppercorns  
   - 3 gm Mace  
   - 3 gm Green cardamom  
   - 2 gm Black cardamom  
   - 2 gm Cloves  
   - 1.5 gm Cinnamon stick  
   - 6-7 Bay leaves  

Set aside **2.5 tsp of this masala** for later use.  

---

### **Step 3: Cook the Beef**  
1. Heat a heavy-bottom pot on medium heat. Add:  
   - 4 tbsp Fried onion oil  
   - 1.5 tbsp Ghee  
   - ¾ inch Cinnamon stick  
   - 2-3 Mace  
   - 3 Green cardamom  
   - 3 Black cardamom  
   - 4-5 Cloves  
   - 3 Bay leaves  

2. Add the marinated beef and cook on high heat for **5-7 minutes** to sear.  
3. Reduce the heat, add **2 tsp prepared biryani masala**, and mix well.  
4. Add **½ cup water**, cover, and cook until the beef is tender (approximately **40-50 minutes** on low heat). Stir occasionally.  

---

### **Step 4: Prepare the Rice**  
1. In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add:  
   - Enough water to cook 1.5 kg Sella basmati rice  
   - Salt to taste  
   - 2 Bay leaves  
   - ¾ inch Cinnamon stick  
   - 3 Black cardamom  
   - 3 Green cardamom  

2. Add **1.5 kg soaked Sella basmati rice** and cook until it’s **70-80% cooked**.  
3. Drain the rice and set it aside.  

---

### **Step 5: Prepare the Jhol (Gravy)**  
In a bowl, mix together:  
- 3 tbsp Fresh mint leaves (chopped)  
- 3 tbsp Fresh coriander leaves (chopped)  
- ⅓ cup Fried onions  
- 3 Green chilies (mild, slit)  
- 1 Green chili (spicy, slit)  
- ⅓ cup Excess oil from the cooked beef  
- 3 tbsp Fresh cream  
- 1.5 tsp Prepared biryani masala  
- Salt to taste  
- 2 tsp Kewra water  

---

### **Step 6: Layer the Biryani**  
1. In a heavy-bottom pot, add the following layers in order:  
   - Half of the cooked beef.  
   - Half of the cooked rice.  
   - Half of the prepared jhol.  
   - 1.5 tsp Prepared biryani masala.  
2. Repeat the process with the remaining beef, rice, jhol, and masala.  
3. Sprinkle on top:  
   - 3 tbsp Saffron water (soak saffron in warm water for 10 minutes)  
   - Fresh mint leaves (a handful)  
   - Fresh coriander leaves (a handful)  
   - Fried onions for garnish  

---

### **Step 7: Dum Cook the Biryani**  
1. Seal the pot with a lid or dough to trap the steam.  
2. Cook on low heat for **25-30 minutes** to allow the flavors to blend.  

---

### **Step 8: Serve**  
Gently mix the biryani before serving. Serve hot with raita or salad.
